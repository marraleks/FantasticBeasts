Newt Scamander

Newton Artemis Fido "Newt" Scamander,[2] O.M. (Second Class), (b. 24 February[1], 1897[2]) was a famed Magizoologist and author of Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them. Early in life Scamander developed an interest in magical creatures, influenced by his mother's breeding of hippogriffs. He attended Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, where he was sorted into Hufflepuff House. While at Hogwarts he was sentenced to expulsion, though Albus Dumbledore, who had been his Defence Against the Dark Arts instructor and recognised his innocence, objected strongly.

What is a beast

«The definition of a ‘beast’ has caused controversy for centuries. Though this might surprise some first-time students of Magizoology, the problem might come into clearer focus if we take a moment to consider three types of magical creature. Werewolves spend most of their time as humans (whether wizard or Muggle). Once a month, however, they transform into savage, four-legged beasts of murderous intent and no human conscience. The centaurs’ habits are not human-like; they live in the wild, refuse clothing, prefer to live apart from wizards and Muggles alike and yet have intelligence equal to theirs.» Excerpt from: J.K. Rowling. «Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them.» Apple Books.

The book

The book ‘Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find’ is a copy of Harry Potter's textbook that was written by Newt Scamander, a magizoologist and fictional character from Series. The book has the same name as previously mentioned in the first novel of Harry Potter series, Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone. The book is a part of syllabus of first year Hogwarts students. The books holds information about mysterious animals or beasts, their lifestyle, habits with special section about what not to feed or be careful about when dealing with or handling any beast. While the book takes you to a journey across five continents in quest to find magical beasts, the handwritten detailing and comments by Harry, Hermione Granger and Ron Weasley on their personal experiences adds light humour to the background.

The author

J. K. Rowling is a British novelist, film producer and screenwriter. She is widely known for the Harry Potter fantasy series, which has sold over 400 million copies and won dozens of prestigious awards. Apart from being a best seller series, her works has earned recognition as the second highest earning film series of the era. This best-selling fiction author of the United Kingdom, has also written three crime fiction novels under the Pseudo name Robert Galbraith: Career of Evil, The Silkworm and The Cookoo's Calling.
